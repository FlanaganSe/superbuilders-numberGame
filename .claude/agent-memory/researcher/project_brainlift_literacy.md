# Literacy Research Findings — Spelling Mode

Research completed 2026-03-14. Full synthesis at `docs/research/literacy-science.md`.
Deep research (Elkonin, encoding, CVC sequence, multimodal, picture dependency, PA sequence) at `docs/research/phonics-deep-dive.md`.

## Core finding
The tile-placement mechanic is already a validated phonological activity (physical Elkonin box). The pedagogy should exploit this explicitly.

## WWC Evidence Ratings (K–3)
- Phonemic awareness instruction: **Strong Evidence** (Rec 2)
- Decoding + Encoding (writing words): **Strong Evidence** (Rec 3)
- Connected text reading: Moderate Evidence (Rec 4)

## Orthographic Mapping (Ehri 2014)
- OM is how words become permanent sight words. Prerequisites: phonemic segmentation/blending + grapheme-phoneme knowledge.
- Encoding (spelling from memory) activates OM; showing the word and having child copy it does NOT.
- **Why:** The memory retrieval step is what creates the bond. Passive copying eliminates it.

## Phonemic Awareness Developmental Sequence
- Age 5: only 29% can blend phonemes; 7% can segment. CVC words are the right difficulty floor.
- Kindergarten: rhyming, alliteration, syllables, introducing phoneme manipulation.
- Grade 1 (age 6–7): full phoneme blending and segmenting expected.

## Four Pillars (Hirsh-Pasek et al. 2015 / Griffith et al. 2021)
73–87% of existing "educational" apps score low on all four pillars. Key failure modes:
- Passive tapping with no thinking required (fails Pillar 1)
- Seductive details / excessive rewards covering screen (fails Pillar 2)
- No corrective/formative feedback — only motivational (fails Pillar 2)

## Recommendation
Option C: Progressive encoding with scaffolded hints.
- Attempt 1: child encodes from spoken word + picture (pure OM)
- Attempt 2: first letter revealed as hint
- Attempt 3: full word shown (still requires physical tile placement)
Maps to existing 3/2/1 star system.

## Word List Principles
- Start: 3-letter CVC decodable words (cat, dog, hat, pig, sun, cup, bed)
- Level 2: 4-letter decodable words (ball, fish, frog, jump)
- Avoid irregular words at early levels (the, was, said)
- Max 3–4 words/session (working memory limit for ages 5–8)

**How to apply:** When designing or evaluating any spelling mode feature, test it against: (a) does the child have to retrieve the spelling from phonological analysis (not copy), (b) is feedback corrective not just motivational, (c) is the word list CVC-first and phonologically appropriate.
